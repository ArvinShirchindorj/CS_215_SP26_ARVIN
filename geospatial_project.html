Mapping Professor Wirfs-Brock’s Interview Visit to Whitman

Introduction

For this project, I worked with Professor Wirfs-Brock’s location data from her interview visit to Whitman in March 2022. The dataset included latitude and longitude points, timestamps, and distance from Olin Hall. Since this was geospatial data, I used maps and distance calculations to understand where her recorded locations were and what patterns appeared in the data.

Part A: Distance Analysis

For Part A, I chose four locations in Walla Walla and used the haversine formula to calculate how far Professor Wirfs-Brock was from these locations. The locations I chose were Reid Campus Center, Bennington Lake, McDonald’s on 9th Ave, and Memorial Building.

Part B: Hypothesis and Exploration

My original hypothesis was that Professor WB’s recorded time in Walla Walla would mostly be centered around Whitman’s campus, especially near Olin Hall, because the dataset came from her interview visit to Whitman.

To test this, I used several visualizations and calculations. I looked at her movement in Walla Walla, the main hotspots of recorded locations, and the distribution of distances from Olin Hall.

Movement in Walla Walla

This visualization shows Professor WB’s recorded movement in Walla Walla. Instead of only showing the points by themselves, this map connects the points in time order. This makes it easier to see the movement pattern across the city.

Hotspot Map

Next, I created a hotspot map of the Walla Walla points. This map shows where the recorded points were most concentrated. I made this because I wanted to look at the data from a different angle, instead of only measuring distance from Olin Hall.

Conclusion

My original hypothesis was that Professor WB’s recorded Walla Walla locations would mostly be centered around Whitman’s campus, especially near Olin Hall. However, the data did not fully support that idea.

The distance summary showed that only 20.67% of the Walla Walla points were within 1 mile of Olin Hall. The analysis also showed that many points were farther away from Olin than I expected. The movement map and hotspot map gave more context by showing that the recorded locations were not only centered around the immediate campus area.
